Conjugate Functions & Fenchel Duality

Re-express convex objectives in dual coordinates to derive elegant bounds and algorithms.

Convex Conjugate

Definition

For a proper function \(f: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R} \cup \{+\infty\}\), the convex conjugate \(f^*\) is

\[f^*(y) = \sup_x \{y^T x - f(x)\}.\]

The conjugate maps from primal variable \(x\) to dual variable \(y\), capturing supporting hyperplanes of \(f\).

Fenchel-Young Inequality

A core identity linking primal and dual views is:

\[f(x) + f^*(y) \ge y^T x.\]

Equality holds when \(y \in \partial f(x)\). This is the bridge between subgradients and dual certificates.

Examples of Conjugates

Quadratic

\(f(x)=\tfrac{1}{2}\|x\|_2^2\) has \(f^*(y)=\tfrac{1}{2}\|y\|_2^2\).

Norm

For \(f(x)=\|x\|\), the conjugate is the indicator of the dual unit ball: \(f^*(y)=I_{\|y\|_*\le 1}(y)\).

Indicator Function

If \(f=I_C\) for a convex set \(C\), then \(f^*(y)=\sigma_C(y)=\sup_{x\in C} y^T x\), the support function of \(C\).

Fenchel Dual Problem

For primal problems of the form

\[\min_x \; f(Ax) + g(x),\]

one dual form is

\[\max_y \; -f^*(y) - g^*(-A^T y).\]

This form appears in proximal splitting, mirror descent interpretations, and many ML models.

Worked Mini Example

From L2-Regularized Linear Objective to Dual

\[\min_x \; c^T x + \tfrac{\lambda}{2}\|x\|_2^2\]

Here, \(g(x)=c^T x + \tfrac{\lambda}{2}\|x\|_2^2\) has conjugate \(g^*(u)=\tfrac{1}{2\lambda}\|u-c\|_2^2\). Conjugates convert regularized primal forms into smooth dual penalties.

Why This Matters in Practice

  • Derives dual objectives systematically instead of ad-hoc algebra.
  • Explains proximal operators through Moreau decompositions.
  • Supports primal-dual algorithms used in large-scale optimization.
  • Provides dual gaps as stopping criteria with theoretical guarantees.
