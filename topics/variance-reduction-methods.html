Variance Reduction Methods (SVRG/SAGA)

Reduce stochastic gradient noise to recover faster finite-sum convergence behavior.

Finite-Sum Structure

\[F(x)=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^N f_i(x).\]

Plain SGD is cheap per iteration but noisy near the solution. Variance reduction reuses finite-sum structure to keep updates informative.

SVRG Idea

At snapshot \(\tilde{x}\), compute full gradient \(\nabla F(\tilde{x})\). Then use corrected stochastic direction

\[g_k = \nabla f_{i_k}(x_k)-\nabla f_{i_k}(\tilde{x})+\nabla F(\tilde{x}).\]

This keeps unbiasedness while shrinking variance as iterates approach the optimum.

SAGA Idea

SAGA stores a memory table of historical component gradients and updates one entry per step, producing low-variance directions without periodic full passes.

Rule of Thumb

Use SVRG when occasional full passes are cheap enough; use SAGA when memory is available and streaming updates matter.

  • Finite-sum datasets repeatedly scanned across epochs.
  • Strongly convex regularized empirical risk objectives.
  • Regimes where SGD plateaus due to gradient noise floor.
