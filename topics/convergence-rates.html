Convergence Rates

Understand sublinear, accelerated, and linear regimes with clear side-by-side comparisons.

Rate Families at a Glance

  • Subgradient (non-smooth convex): often \(O(1/\sqrt{k})\).
  • Gradient descent (smooth convex): \(O(1/k)\).
  • Accelerated smooth methods: \(O(1/k^2)\).
  • Strongly convex with proper steps: linear \(O(\rho^k)\), \(0<\rho<1\).

Ergodic vs Non-ergodic

Many primal-dual and splitting algorithms provide rate guarantees for averaged iterates (ergodic) before pointwise guarantees (non-ergodic) are available.

Worked Example

How Big Is The Gap After 100 Iterations?

Suppose the initial gap is \(g_0=1\) and \(\kappa=25\). Then \(\rho=(\kappa-1)/(\kappa+1)=24/26\approx 0.9231\).

\[g_{100}^{\text{linear}}\approx 0.9231^{100}\approx 3.3\times 10^{-4},\]

while benchmark sublinear models give \(g_{100}^{1/k}\approx 10^{-2}\), \(g_{100}^{1/k^2}\approx 10^{-4}\), \(g_{100}^{1/\sqrt{k}}\approx 10^{-1}\). This gives a quick, practical sense of algorithm class separation.
