Operator Splitting Methods

Decompose hard composite problems into simple gradient and proximal sub-operators.

Composite Objective Pattern

\[\min_x\; f(x) + g(x),\]

with smooth \(f\) and possibly non-smooth \(g\). Splitting applies different updates to each part.

Forward-Backward (Prox-Gradient)

\[x^{k+1}=\operatorname{prox}_{\alpha g}(x^k-\alpha\nabla f(x^k)).\]

A forward (gradient) step for \(f\), then backward (prox) step for \(g\).

Douglas-Rachford / ADMM Connection

Reflection-based splitting in dual or product spaces can lead to ADMM-like practical algorithms. This unifies several methods under monotone operator fixed-point perspectives.

Worked Example

Single Forward-Backward Step With L1 Regularization

Let \(f(x)=\tfrac{1}{2}(x-2)^2\), \(g(x)=\lambda |x|\), \(\lambda=0.4\), \(\alpha=0.5\), and \(x^0=3\). Gradient step:

\[v = x^0-\alpha\nabla f(x^0)=3-0.5(3-2)=2.5.\]

Prox step for \(\alpha g\) is soft-thresholding with threshold \(\alpha\lambda=0.2\):

\[x^1 = \operatorname{sign}(v)\max(|v|-0.2,0)=2.3.\]

This illustrates splitting: smooth descent from \(f\), then sparsity-inducing shrinkage from \(g\).
