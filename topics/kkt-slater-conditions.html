KKT Conditions and Slater's Condition

Connect primal feasibility, stationarity, and complementary slackness to certify optimality.

Convex Program Template

\[\min_x f(x) \quad \text{s.t. } g_i(x) \le 0,\; Ax=b.\]

With convex \(f\), convex \(g_i\), and affine equalities, KKT conditions characterize optimal solutions under standard regularity assumptions.

KKT System

  • Primal feasibility: \(g_i(x^*)\le 0\), \(Ax^*=b\)
  • Dual feasibility: \(\lambda_i^*\ge 0\)
  • Stationarity: \(\nabla f(x^*) + \sum_i \lambda_i^* \nabla g_i(x^*) + A^T\nu^*=0\)
  • Complementary slackness: \(\lambda_i^* g_i(x^*)=0\)

Slater's Condition

If there exists a strictly feasible point \(\bar{x}\) such that \(g_i(\bar{x})<0\) for all inequality constraints, then strong duality holds for convex programs with affine equalities.

Why It Matters

Slater's condition is the bridge from dual lower bounds to exact primal values, making KKT certificates complete.

Checklist for New Models

  • Write all constraints in canonical \(g_i(x)\le0\) form.
  • Check strict feasibility early to justify strong duality claims.
  • Track stationarity and complementarity residuals in your solver logs.
