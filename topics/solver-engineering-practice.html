Solver Engineering Practice

Turn theory into robust optimization pipelines with practical diagnostics and control rules.

Core Engineering Checklist

  • Scaling: normalize columns and constraint magnitudes.
  • Stopping: combine objective, feasibility, and stationarity criteria.
  • Warm starts: reuse previous solutions across similar runs.
  • Safeguards: cap steps, detect stagnation, and restart when needed.

Typical Stop Rule

A practical stop condition checks several residuals together:

\[\|r_{\text{prim}}\|\le\varepsilon_p,\quad \|r_{\text{dual}}\|\le\varepsilon_d,\quad \|\nabla f\|\le\varepsilon_g.\]

Worked Example

Stopping Decision With Multi-Metric Thresholds

Assume tolerances \(\varepsilon_p=10^{-4}\), \(\varepsilon_d=10^{-4}\), \(\varepsilon_g=5\times 10^{-5}\) and current diagnostics \(\|r_{\text{prim}}\|=8\times 10^{-5}\), \(\|r_{\text{dual}}\|=1.6\times 10^{-4}\), \(\|\nabla f\|=3\times 10^{-5}\).

Two checks pass, but dual feasibility fails. Practical action: continue iterations, or tighten scaling/preconditioning on dual blocks before rerunning. This prevents false convergence when objective looks flat but KKT consistency is incomplete.
