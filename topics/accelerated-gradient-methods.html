Accelerated Gradient Methods

Add momentum in a principled way to improve first-order convergence on smooth convex objectives.

Why Acceleration?

Plain gradient descent can be conservative on ill-conditioned landscapes. Nesterov-style acceleration introduces a look-ahead point that combines current gradients with controlled momentum.

Strongly Convex Nesterov Update

For \(\mu\)-strongly convex, \(L\)-smooth objectives:

\[y_k = x_k + \beta(x_k - x_{k-1}), \quad x_{k+1}=y_k-\frac{1}{L}\nabla f(y_k),\]

with \(\beta = (\sqrt{\kappa}-1)/(\sqrt{\kappa}+1)\), \(\kappa=L/\mu\).

FISTA Connection

For composite problems \(f(x)+g(x)\), FISTA applies Nesterov-style momentum to proximal gradient steps and often improves practical convergence dramatically. 

repeat
    y_k = x_k + beta_k * (x_k - x_{k-1})
    x_{k+1} = prox_{alpha g}(y_k - alpha * grad f(y_k))
until convergence

Practice Notes

  • Acceleration is sensitive to noise; consider restart heuristics.
  • For composite models, combine with proximal operators (FISTA).
  • Track both objective and gradient norm to avoid misleading progress signals.
