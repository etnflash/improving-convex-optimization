Mirror Descent & Bregman Divergence

Use problem geometry instead of Euclidean distance to design better first-order updates.

Why Mirror Descent?

Standard gradient descent measures progress with Euclidean distance. But many constrained domains, such as probability simplices, have non-Euclidean geometry. Mirror descent adapts updates to this geometry and often gives cleaner guarantees.

Bregman Divergence

Definition

Let \(\psi\) be a differentiable, strictly convex mirror map. The Bregman divergence is

\[D_{\psi}(x,y)=\psi(x)-\psi(y)-\nabla\psi(y)^T(x-y).\]

It is always nonnegative, but generally not symmetric.

Mirror Descent Update

For minimizing convex \(f\) over a convex set \(C\), a common form is

\[x_{k+1}=\arg\min_{x\in C}\left\{\eta_k g_k^T x + D_{\psi}(x,x_k)\right\},\]

where \(g_k\in\partial f(x_k)\) and \(\eta_k\) is a step size.

Dual-Space View

Mirror descent can be read as a gradient step in dual coordinates:

\[\nabla\psi(x_{k+1}) = \nabla\psi(x_k) - \eta_k g_k,\]

followed by mapping back to primal space through \((\nabla\psi)^{-1}\).

Classic Example: Entropy Mirror Map

Simplex Optimization

On the simplex, choose \(\psi(x)=\sum_i x_i\log x_i\). Then \(D_\psi\) becomes KL divergence, and mirror descent yields multiplicative weights / exponentiated-gradient style updates.

This geometry respects positivity and normalization naturally, unlike naive Euclidean steps.

When to Use It

  • Probability vectors, simplex constraints, and distributions.
  • High-dimensional sparse problems where geometry-aware bounds help.
  • Online convex optimization and regret-minimization settings.
  • Primal-dual schemes related to Bregman proximal updates.

Practice Checklist

  • Compare Euclidean projection vs KL-based mirror updates on a simplex toy problem.
  • Derive the update for entropy mirror map with linear loss.
  • Verify that \(D_{\psi}(x,y)\ge 0\) numerically for random points.
  • Relate mirror descent to proximal point updates in dual space.
