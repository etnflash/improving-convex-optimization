Proximal Operators & Moreau Envelope

Solve non-smooth convex problems with stable updates and smooth surrogate geometry.

Why Proximal Methods?

Gradient descent struggles on non-smooth terms such as \(\|x\|_1\) or indicator constraints. Proximal updates replace a difficult function with a local quadratic + exact regularizer solve, giving robust steps for composite objectives.

Composite Template

\[\min_x \; F(x)=f(x)+g(x),\]

where \(f\) is smooth and \(g\) is convex, possibly non-smooth.

Proximal Operator

Definition

For \(\lambda>0\), the proximal map of \(g\) is

\[\operatorname{prox}_{\lambda g}(v)=\arg\min_x\left\{g(x)+\frac{1}{2\lambda}\|x-v\|_2^2\right\}.\]

Intuition: find a point that balances low \(g(x)\) and staying close to \(v\).

Proximal Gradient Step

For \(F(x)=f(x)+g(x)\), one step with step size \(\alpha\) is

\[x_{k+1}=\operatorname{prox}_{\alpha g}\left(x_k-\alpha\nabla f(x_k)\right).\]

This is often called ISTA when \(g=\lambda\|x\|_1\), and accelerated versions lead to FISTA.

Classic Example: Soft-Thresholding

For \(g(x)=\lambda\|x\|_1\), the prox is coordinate-wise shrinkage:

\[\operatorname{prox}_{\alpha\lambda\|\cdot\|_1}(v)_i= \operatorname{sign}(v_i)\max(|v_i|-\alpha\lambda,0).\]

Why It Matters

The soft-threshold map is exactly the sparsity mechanism in Lasso and many signal processing models.

Moreau Envelope

Smooth Surrogate

The Moreau envelope of \(g\) with parameter \(\lambda\) is

\[e_{\lambda}g(v)=\min_x\left\{g(x)+\frac{1}{2\lambda}\|x-v\|_2^2\right\}.\]

It smooths \(g\) while preserving minimizers in useful settings.

A key relation is

\[\nabla e_{\lambda}g(v)=\frac{1}{\lambda}\left(v-\operatorname{prox}_{\lambda g}(v)\right).\]

This gives a differentiable handle on originally non-smooth geometry.

Moreau Decomposition

Proximal maps of a function and its conjugate are linked by

\[v = \operatorname{prox}_{\lambda g}(v) + \lambda\operatorname{prox}_{g^*/\lambda}(v/\lambda).\]

This identity is central in primal-dual splitting and conjugate-based modeling.
