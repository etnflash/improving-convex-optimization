Conic Optimization

Unify LP, QP, SOCP, and SDP models using cone constraints and linear maps.

Canonical Conic Form

\[\min_x c^Tx \quad \text{s.t. } Ax=b,\; x \in \mathcal{K}.\]

The cone \(\mathcal{K}\) determines the model class. Different choices recover many practical convex programs.

Cone Families

  • LP: nonnegative orthant cone \(\mathbb{R}_+^n\).
  • SOCP: second-order (Lorentz) cones \(\{(t,z):\|z\|_2\le t\}\).
  • SDP: positive semidefinite cone \(S_+^n\).
  • Exponential / Power cones: entropy and geometric constraints.

Dual Perspective

Conic duality has the same shape with the dual cone \(\mathcal{K}^*\). For self-dual cones (e.g., \(\mathbb{R}_+^n\), Lorentz, and PSD cones), primal and dual geometry align elegantly.
